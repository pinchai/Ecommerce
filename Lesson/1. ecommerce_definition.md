# What is E-Commerce?

## Definition

**E-commerce (Electronic Commerce)** is the buying and selling of goods or services through the internet. It allows businesses and customers to conduct transactions online using websites, mobile applications, or online marketplaces.

Customers can browse products, place orders, make payments, and receive products or services without visiting a physical store.

> E-commerce is the process of conducting business transactions electronically over the internet.

---

# Examples of E-Commerce

E-commerce includes many types of online business activities, such as:

- Online shopping websites
- Mobile shopping apps
- Online marketplaces
- Digital product sales
- Online service platforms

## Common Examples

- L192 – Online shopping platform (Cambodia)
- Khmer24 – Online auction and marketplace (Cambodia)
- Facebook & Telegram – Social commerce and online advertising
- PassApp / Grab / Nham24 – Ride-sharing and delivery platforms
- Businesses selling software, e-books, music, or online courses

---

# Types of E-Commerce

## 1. Business to Consumer (B2C)

Businesses sell directly to customers.

### Examples
- Online clothing stores
- Electronics websites

## 2. Business to Business (B2B)

Businesses sell products or services to other businesses.

### Examples
- Wholesale suppliers
- Enterprise software providers

## 3. Consumer to Consumer (C2C)

Consumers sell products to other consumers.

### Examples
- Online marketplaces
- Second-hand product platforms

## 4. Consumer to Business (C2B)

Individuals provide products or services to companies.

### Examples
- Freelancers offering services online

- Recommend products

# Advantages of E-Commerce

## For Customers

- Shop anytime and anywhere
- Wide variety of products
- Easy price comparison
- Faster purchasing process

## For Businesses

- Global market reach
- Lower operational costs
- Better customer data analysis
- Increased sales opportunities

---

# Disadvantages of E-Commerce

- Security and privacy concerns
- No physical inspection of products
- Delivery delays
- Dependence on internet access
- Technical issues and cyber threats

---

# Conclusion

E-commerce has transformed the way businesses and consumers interact. It enables fast, convenient, and global transactions through digital platforms. As technologies like AI, mobile payments, and automation continue to grow, e-commerce will become even more important in modern business and everyday life.
